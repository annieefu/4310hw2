How Sustainable is your U.S. Grocery List?

In an era of nearly irreversible climate change and increased need for sustainability, one of the most accessible avenues we have for accessing change is through reflection of our habits, especially in personal resource and waste management. However, no two countries are alike in geography, agriculture, or climate, and as a result, the data regarding agricultural sustainability varies greatly depending on where you live. As seen on the visualization, of the top twenty most populous countries with available data, the United States ranks in the middle for overall sustainability, but what about for individual factors relating to water?

You may have noticed that the U.S. falls either near the middle or the top for all these categories, except for Water Footprint. Why does this occur? A major contributor to US's water footprint is through what agricultural products we grow and consume. Water footprint is usually broken down into three categories: green, blue, grey. Green water footprint is water from precipitation that is stored in the root zone of the soil. Blue water footprint is water that has been sourced from surface or groundwater resources. Grey water footprint is the amount of fresh water required to assimilate pollutants to meet specific water quality standards.
